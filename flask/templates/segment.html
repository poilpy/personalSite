Semantic Image Segmentation

For my artificial inteligence class at UC Santa Cruz I wanted to do a project about a long interest of mine, Image Segmentation, but also a project that was managable and captured the spirit of the course. So I decided to implement the ideas of Johnathan Long's paper on fully convolutional neural networks. My interest in image segmentation began in my junior college's honors program where I did a research project on the power and applications of image segmentation; whether it be medical imaging to detect tumors or other issues in medical scans that professionals don't have the time or skill to detect on their own, or in autonomous vehicles where cameras are one of many tools to help vehicles detects objects around them. I've always seen machine learning as one of the biggest technological revolutions since the industrial revolution. Just as the industrial revolution gave humanity the ability to automate physical labor, machine learning gives humanity the ability to automate intellectual labor. Computer vision gives machines the ability to see and understand the world around them.

What is the purpose?

Self-driving Cars

Through your windshield a typical view may consist of street lamps, traffic lights, other cars, pedestrians, barriers and the sidewalk. The difference between all of these items is crucial, and an autonomous vehicle needs to understand them with a very high accuracy. These segmentations help create an understanding of the world around the vehicle.

Cancer Detection

Early detection of cancerous cells is vital in saving someone's life. In these life or death situations, humans may not be qualified to look through every image of cells and make an accurate estimate of the state of a disease. By using Image Segmentation on medical images, diseases may be found at early stages more accurately.
